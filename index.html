Neural Network Based Obstavle Aware Trajectory Generation for UAVs

I developed a real-time, neural network-based motion planning framework to enable collision-free navigation in obstacle-dense environments. The framework is platform-agnostic and applicable to both aerial and ground-based autonomous systems. I implemented and validated the system on a quadcopter equipped with a Jetson Nano, Pixhawk 6C flight controller, and Intel RealSense D435 stereo camera.

The guidance module combines deep learning with optimization-based trajectory generation. I trained three neural network models using convex-optimized trajectories:

  • NN-Parameter (NN-P): Predicts obstacle-aware waypoints with time and velocity information.
  • NN-Segment (NN-S): Generates B-spline-based segment trajectories between waypoints.
  • NN-Trajectory (NN-T): Predicts full trajectory coefficients directly.
The neural network models were optimized using Optuna.

For perception, I integrated YOLOv3-Tiny with a stereo camera using ROS to build an onboard object detection and localization (ODL) pipeline. Obstacles were detected in real time and localized in an inertial frame. In addition, I developed a monocular camera-based human localization algorithm capable of estimating relative position, velocity, and uncertainty without using depth estimation and suitable for resource-constrained platforms.

Experimental flight tests revealed the guidance and perception system's effectiveness in real-time obstacle avoidance and path execution.

Speed and Phase Synchronizing Controllers for Multi-Rotors

The phase offset between propellers affect the noise and vibrations in aerial vehicles that use multi-rotor systems. In this work, I developed and validated a phase controller for multi-rotor systems. I tested the proposed phase control algorithm across various propeller configurations, including a single propeller, side-by-side propeller, and co-axial propellers. To evaluate the effectiveness of the phase controller, I developed a post-validation algorithm using a high-speed imaging system coupled with image processing techniques. A propeller cross-over control algorithm was developed for counter-rotating, mechanically decoupled multirotor systems to ensure that they cross over at a given reference angular position.

Keywords: LabVIEW, Arduino, Python, MATLAB, PID Control, High Speed Imaging.

Global Trajectory Optimization Competition

I participated in the 12th Global Trajectory Optimization Competition (https://gtoc12.tsinghua.edu.cn/competition/theProblem) as a member of Team ACELab, where we secured 20th place out of 102 teams. The competition posed a challenging asteroid mining scenario: "In the future around 2035, humanity plans to mine asteroids. Using Sustainable Asteroid Miners, we can autonomously extract and refine valuable minerals. This ambitious 15-year mission involves launching Mining Ships from Earth, rendezvousing with asteroids to deploy a miner or retrieve the collected minerals, and transporting them back to Earth. The primary objective of the GTOC12 problem is to maximize the return of minerals to Earth. To encourage early solution submissions and the mining of rarely explored asteroids, a game problem is adopted with a dynamic bonus coefficient, decreasing with the mass mined from each asteroid. The challenge is not only to maximize mineral retrieval but also to ensure all Mining Ships work cooperatively. Gravity assists at Venus, Earth, and Mars may also play a significant role". My contributions included, 1. Developing a low-level optimizer using indirect optimization methods to compute optimal transfer trajectories to asteroids, ensuring fuel-efficient maneuvers, 2. Developing a high-level optimizer using direct optimization techniques to maximize the mass collected from each asteroid while considering mission constraints and spacecraft capabilities.

Technologies Used: Optimization Algorithms, Trajectory Planning, Mass allocation

Uncertainty Aware Desensitized Optimal Control Formulation

Nonlinear optimal control problems (OCPs) often exhibit dispersion under model uncertainties, making it crucial to generate optimal solutions that are less sensitive to these uncertainties. In this work, I propose a novel solution desensitization method, the Reduced Desensitization Formulation (RDF) when a hybrid indirect-direct method is used. A key advantage of the RDF method is its ability to significantly reduce the number of differential equations required for generating desensitized solutions. This feature enables the efficient computation of open-loop desensitized trajectories, making the approach applicable to OCPs with a larger number of uncertain parameters. Additionally, I introduced a time-triggered desensitization mechanism by modifying the RDF method. Instead of desensitizing the entire trajectory, I selectively apply desensitization to specific segments, thereby improving the optimality of the desensitized trajectories while maintaining robustness to model uncertainties.

Keywords: Non-Linear Optimization, Optimal Control Theory, Space Trajectory Optimization.

State Estimation

In this project, I developed a system for real-time attitude and position estimation using a 9-axis IMU (MPU9250) and GPS (NEO-M8N). The IMU data (accelerometer, gyroscope, and magnetometer) was processed using a Kalman filter to estimate the orientation (roll, pitch, and yaw) of the system, while the GPS data provided latitude, longitude, and altitude for position tracking. The Kalman filter implementation included bias calibration for both the accelerometer and gyroscope to improve accuracy. Additionally, the GPS data was transformed into a local North-East-Down (NED) coordinate frame for easier interpretation and integration with other navigation systems.

Keywords: Arduino, IMU, GPS, Kalman Filter, State Estimation.

Minimum Snap Trajectory Generator with Time Allocation

During this project, I developed and analyzed optimal trajectory generation and control strategies for quadcopters, focusing on minimum jerk and minimum snap polynomial trajectories. The two polynomial trajectory genaration approach were also compared to study the trade-offs in implementing them. To optimize flight efficiency I also incorporated time allocation techniques. Additionally, I designed and implemented a PD controller to track the generated trajectories. The simulation results demonstrated that while minimum snap trajectories provide smoother motion, they tend to overshoot near waypoints requiring careful planning for obstacle avoidance.

Keywords: Quadcopter, Trajectory Planning, PD Controller, Time Allocation.

Deep Q-Learning

In this project, I implemented a Deep Q-Network (DQN) to solve the OpenAI Gym Lunar Lander problem, simulating a controlled rocket landing. The problem was formulated using reinforcement learning, where the lander received rewards based on soft landings and penalties for crashes. Initially, I experimented with different neural network architectures and hyperparameters, testing variations in hidden layers, learning rates, and discount factors to optimize performance. To improve training stability and efficiency, I incorporated experience replay and a target network, which significantly enhanced the learning process. Through iterative tuning, I achieved a trained model that successfully landed the spacecraft consistently between the target flags. The study highlighted the importance of hyperparameter selection in reinforcement learning and provided insights into optimizing DQN models for control applications.

Keywords: Python, PyTorch, Reinforcement Learning.

Robust Costate Initialization for Optimal Control Problems

I apply the variational approach to optimal control theory, which transforms trajectory optimization problems into two- or multiple-point boundary-value problems involving costates (i.e., Lagrange multipliers associated with the states). Estimating the missing values of these non-intuitive costates is a crucial step in solving the resulting boundary-value problems. By leveraging the costate vector mapping theorem, I extend the Adjoint Control Transformation (ACT) method, introducing Mapped ACT (MACT), which allows for solving low-thrust trajectory optimization problems in alternative coordinate systems. Specifically, I extend ACT to modified equinoctial elements and an orbital element set based on specific angular momentum and eccentricity vectors (h-e set). To evaluate the computational efficiency and robustness of the MACT method, I compare it against the traditionally used random initialization of costates by solving interplanetary rendezvous maneuvers and an Earth-centered orbit-raising problem, with and without J‚ÇÇ perturbation. Numerical results show that MACT improves the convergence rate of boundary-value problems by a factor of two to three compared to random initialization. Additionally, results indicate that the h-e set is a promising and effective choice for solving low-thrust trajectory optimization problems.

Keywords: Space Trajectory Optimization, Optimal Control Theory, Robust Initialization, Indirect Optimization.

Development of Multi Rotor Simulation Environments in Gazebo

During this internship, I developed a hexacopter simulation model in Gazebo and integrated it with PX4 for realistic flight dynamics and control testing. The project involved designing an SDF model representing a hexacopter with accurate inertia, mass distribution, and motor configurations. I defined collision and physical properties to ensure accurate physics simulation. For integration with PX4, I configured motor mappings and joint definitions to ensure the hexacopter‚Äôs simulated actuators responded correctly to PX4 commands. I then tuned inertial properties and control parameters for stable flight and conducted test flights in Gazebo to improve stability and maneuverability. Additionally, I developed a Gazebo world featuring oil tanks and industrial structures to provide pilots with a realistic training environment for navigation and inspection tasks, adjusting environmental physics such as wind and lighting conditions to simulate real-world scenarios.

Technologies Used: Gazebo, PX4
